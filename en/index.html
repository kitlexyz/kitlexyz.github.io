Support & Privacy

Ksteps is built to help you check today's step count and goal progress at a glance on Apple Watch. This page provides support information and the privacy policy for Ksteps.

Support

About the App

Ksteps is designed to help you quickly view your step count on Apple Watch and complications. You can configure your step goal, language, and unit preferences, and the app reads Apple Health step data to show your progress. On Apple Watch, you can also start and save walking or hiking workout sessions.

Things to Check

  • When you open the app for the first time on Apple Watch, an Apple HealthKit permission prompt may appear.
  • It can take a few seconds for the permission sheet to finish responding.
  • Your latest step count may refresh when you open or refresh the Apple Watch app.

Privacy Policy

Information We Access

Ksteps may read Apple HealthKit step data, such as your daily step count, to display it in the app and complications. If you start a workout on Apple Watch, the app may save that walking or hiking workout record to HealthKit.

How the Information Is Used

  • To display your step count and goal progress in the app and Apple Watch complications.
  • To save walking or hiking workout sessions that you explicitly start on Apple Watch to Apple Health.
  • To store preferences such as your step goal, language, unit, and complication title in local device storage and the app group container.

Server Transmission

Ksteps does not send HealthKit data to the developer's servers and does not store it separately on external systems. Workout record saving happens on your device and within the Apple Health ecosystem.

Third-Party Sharing

Ksteps does not share your HealthKit data with third parties.
